Arunachal Pradesh (Itanagar) - The Land of The Rising Sun

Itanagar, 'the land of the dawn lit mountains' is the capital city of north eastern state Arunachal Pradesh.
It is nestled on the foothills of Himalayas.
Blessed with pampering surroundings and pleasant climate, Itanagar flaunts quite a number of attractions.
Itanagar offers immense satisfaction to its tourists in terms of pleasurable holidays.
Temples, Churches, lakes, ruins of palace and fort are some of its highlights which speaks oodles about its glorious past.
In the 11th century it was the capital of the Jitari dynasty.
Tibetan culture is deeply interwoven into the lives of the people here.
Be it costumes or festivals, the influence of the Tibetan culture is clearly visible.
In fact, Itanagar is a land where people from different cultural backgrounds live in perfect harmony

Tourist Attraction

Situated at the foothills of Himalayas, Itanagar is the capital of Arunachal Pradesh. Pleasant climate, scenic vistas and naturally rich environment make Itanagar a perfect destination for long vacations.
Housing a large population of the Nyishi tribe, Itanagar offers a wonderful experience with its unique culture and hospitable locals.

Itanagar has a rich mixture of archeological sites of great historical value and built up resources and institutions of great socio-cultural significance.
Itafort, ‘the fort of bricks’ from which the State capital derives its name, is situated in the heart of the Itanagar.
The fort has an irregular shape, built mainly with bricks dating back to the 14th-15th Century of Mayapur of Ramachandra, a king of the Jitari Dynasty.
Geykar Sinyik (Ganga Lake) is a beautiful natural lake locally known as Geykar Sinyik surrounded by landmass of hard rock.
The name Gekar Sinyi means ‘confined water’.
Jawaharlal Nehru Museum has a rich collection of textiles, weaponry, household articles and handicrafts items reflecting the tribal culture and heritage of Arunachal Pradesh.
Craft Centre & Emporium at Itanagar is worth visiting where one can see and buy local artifacts like wall paintings, traditional costumes made of natural vegetable and herbal dye yarns, and attractive utility items made of cane and bamboo.
Zoological Park (Zoo) is located at Chimpu. The park has a variety of fauna including avi-fauna species.
Indira Gandhi Park is a popular park located at Itanagar
Polo Park is a small botanical garden located on top of a ridge at Naharlagun (the twin capital city).
